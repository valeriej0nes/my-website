Who am I?

I’m Valerie, a final-year Mathematics student at the University of Bath with a lifelong love for numbers and problem-solving. Maths has always been my way of understanding the world - it’s the language that explains patterns, uncovers hidden connections, and turns complexity into clarity. Along the way, I discovered data science, and it felt like the perfect bridge between theory and impact. Suddenly, the abstract beauty of maths could be applied to real-world challenges - from predicting engineering parameters to optimising workflows - across industries I’d never imagined working in. What excites me most is the mix of creativity and logic: asking the right questions, shaping messy datasets into meaningful insights, and building models that can genuinely make a difference.

Beyond academics, I’m an active member of the University’s rowing team, where I combine my love of sport with my passion for data. I enjoy applying performance analytics to training - tracking metrics, visualising trends, and using statistical insights to refine technique and strategy. Whether on the water or at my desk, I’m motivated by curiosity, adaptability, and the belief that data, when interpreted well, can inspire improvement and deliver meaningful results.

My Experience

In 2024-2025 I went on a placement year at GE Aerospace, I immersed myself in the challenge of applying machine learning to high-dimensional time series and sensor data from aircraft engines. I built and evaluated supervised learning models - including Random Forest, MLP, and KNN - optimising performance through hyperparameter tuning and feature importance analysis. I also explored Bayesian networks for anomaly detection, integrated SQL queries into large-scale cloud-based pipelines, and used PySpark to streamline model development. Beyond the technical skills, I learned how to work within a rigorous engineering environment - collaborating across teams, maintaining high coding standards, and automating workflows to save significant time for downstream analysts.

In the summer before that, I joined Edwards Vacuum as a Data Analyst Intern in their Business & Logistics department, where I developed algorithms to flag inefficiencies in product transport, leading to tangible cost-saving actions across global operations. I also introduced machine learning to predict customer behaviour, sparking internal discussions about integrating advanced analytics into forecasting practices. This role sharpened my ability to translate technical results into clear, business-oriented recommendations for executives.

Alongside industry experience, my Mathematics degree at the University of Bath has given me a solid foundation of many subjects such as linear algebra, statistics, numerical analysis, and machine learning. I’ve learned to approach problems methodically, balance precision with efficiency, and adapt theoretical knowledge to practical scenarios. The combination of mathematical rigour and hands-on programming has prepared me to tackle complex analytical challenges with both technical depth and creative problem-solving.
